Ateneo de Zamboanga University

The Ateneo de Zamboanga University (ADZU) was established as a parochial school by the Spanish Jesuits in 1912 and was first named Escuela Catolica. It was four years later that the school got its name as Ateneo de Zamboanga. It was declared a university by the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) on August 20, 2001. It was also one of the thirty higher education institutions in the country that were granted Fully Autonomous status. This status is a symbol of excellence and consistency in delivering good education, research and community involvement.

Located at the heart of the Zamboanga Peninsula with constituents of diverse cultural backgrounds, ADZU envisions peace, progress, solidarity, good governance and patriotic citizenship for Mindanao and Philippines as a whole.

Its mission is anchored on its slogan, “pro Deo et Patria”, which means for God and country. It is committed in forming students to become leaders with Filpino, Catholic and Jesuit values who will dedicate themselves to serve God and their fellow countrymen.

ADZU offers basic, higher and professional education with its three separate campuses. The main campus, Fr. Eusebio Salvador, S. J. Campus, houses the undergraduate, graduate and senior high school. The Fr. William H. Kreutz, S. J. Campus in Tumaga houses both the grade school and junior high school. The Lantaka Campus, which was previously a resort hotel, was recently added for educational, religious and social involvement.

The university also strongly advocates research and social outreach. Research activities are handled by the University Research Office (URO) and supervised by the University Research and Publication Council (URPC). Social development initiatives are coursed through the ADZU Social Development Council, which has an institute and three centers: Ateneo Peace Institute (API), Center for Community Extension Services (CCES), Social Awareness and Community Service Involvement (SACSI), and Ateneo Center for Leadership and Governance (ACLG).

University of the Cordilleras

The University of the Cordilleras (UC)), formerly known as Baguio Colleges Foundation (BCF), is the first higher education institution in Northern Luzon founded in 1946. Today, the university caters to more than 18,000 students coming from the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR), Regions I and II, and neighboring provinces of Luzon. In addition, UC has the largest population of international students among HEIs in Baguio City represented by 37 nationalities. Programs offered now range from basic education to graduate studies, law, arts and sciences, business and accountancy, nursing, teacher education, information technology and computer science, hospitality and tourism, and criminal justice.

UC has received recognitions which are testaments to its delivery of quality educational services. In 2019, UC's was hailed ISO 9001:2015 Certified. The Commission on Higher Education has granted UC the Autonomous University Status. The commission has likewise identified three (3) Centers of Excellence in UC namely Information Technology, Criminology, and Teacher Education. The University's College of Law is rated among the top law schools in the country, notably having graduated two (2) Bar Examination First Placers. Additionally, over 20 programs have been accredited by private accrediting bodies.

The University of the Cordilleras is a student-friendly learning venue at the heart of City of Baguio, in the mountain highland region of the Cordillera.



University of San Carlos

University of San Carlos is a private Catholic research university in Cebu City, Philippines which is administered by the Society of the Divine Word (Societas Verbi Divini, SVD) missionaries since 1935. USC provides the educational package from kindergarten, including a Montessori academy, to graduate school. USC offers 7 undergraduate and graduate studies in higher education such as (1) Architecture, Fine Arts and Design; (2) Arts and Sciences; (3) Business and Economics; (4) Education; (5) Engineering; (6) Healthcare Professions; and (7) Law and Governance.

USC consists of five campuses in different areas of Metro Cebu – the Downtown Campus (formerly the Main Campus) in 1935; the North Campus (formerly the Boys High Campus) in 1956; the Talamban Campus (TC) in 1964;the South Campus (formerly the Girls High) in 1964; and the newest is the Montessori Academy at the back of USC North Campus in 2008. The total land area of the University's five campuses is almost 88 hectares (or 217 acres), with about 78 hectares in Talamban Campus alone and potentially ample room for future expansion.

The University is one of the most respected higher education institutions in the Philippines, offering 45 undergraduate and 62 graduate programs. Many of these programs have received Level II or Level III accreditation from the Federation of Accrediting Associations of the Philippines (FAAP), as evaluated by the Philippine Accrediting Association of Schools, Colleges, and Universities (PAASCU). Six engineering programs are also accredited by the Philippine Technological Council-Accreditation and Certification Board for Engineering and Technology (PTC-ACBET). The Philippine Commission on Higher Education (CHED) also granted Autonomous Status to the University, and designated eight Centers of Excellence (COE) and 12 Centers of Development (COD) in USC. Nearly 22,000 students in basic to graduate education are enrolled in the University, with almost 200 international students. On average, the teacher-to-student ratio at USC is 1:20.

The university aims to develop competent and socially responsible professionals and lifelong learners in an environment that fosters excellence in the academic core processes of teaching-learning, research, and community extension service. Its mission is to provide timely, relevant, and transformative academic programs responsive to the needs of the local, national and global communities in a rapidly changing world.



University of the Philippines Cebu

The University of the Philippines Cebu (Cebuano: Unibersidad sa Pilipinas sa Sugbo) is the youngest constituent university of the University of the Philippines System situated in Cebu City, Cebu,Philippines. It was founded on May 3, 1918, ten years after the founding of UP in 1908. It had undergone many name changes through a 100 -year history from Junior College of Liberal Arts in 1918 to Cebu College in UP in 1947, to the UP Graduate School in Cebu in 1963, to the UP Visayas Cebu College in 1987, to the UP Cebu College in 2010, and finally to University of the Philippines Cebu.

With its history at its heart, UP’s youngest constituent unit has embraced the modern, the artistic and the high-tech, as befits its role as one of the country’s centers of excellence in design and information technology. For example, the Department of Trade and Industry inaugurated the first Fabrication Laboratory (FabLab) in Cebu last year under the UP Cebu’s College of Communication, Art and Design. The Fablab is a service facility established for UP Cebu’s Fine Arts program. It aims to give arts and design students, professionals, entrepreneurs, and the public access to advance prototyping, printing and related equipment, as well as training and workshop facilities.



University of the Philippines Diliman

The University of the Philippines (UP) was established in 1908 through Act No. 1870 of the Philippine Assembly. It is the country’s national university as declared by RA 9500 which was approved by the 14th Congress of the Philippines on April 29, 2008. UP System is composed of seven constituent universities and one autonomous college with fifteen (15) campuses in the whole country. Based on the latest Quacquarelli Symonds (QS) World University Rankings, UP lands in the 356th spot in 2019, remaining as the only Philippine institution of higher learning in the top 400 universities in the world.

On April 23, 1985, UP Diliman was formally declared a constituent university (CU). UP Diliman (UPD) is the largest constituent unit of the national university with a campus that covers a 493-hectare land area located in Quezon City. UPD offers the most number of undergraduate and graduate degree programs in the UP System, and has one of the most diversified program offerings in the Philippines. UPD leads in the advancement of science and technology in the country, continues to enrich the Philippine culture and arts, and leads in responding to the needs of the nation and the global community for sustainable development and social transformation. Its population is composed of almost half of the entire UP System student population.

It also houses the Southeast Asian Ministers of Education Organization Regional Center for Educational Innovation and Technology (SEAMEO INNOTECH). It also administers the UP Diliman Extension Program in Pampanga (UPDEPP) which is located at the Clark Special Economic Zone.



University of the Philippines Mindanao

The University of the Philippines Mindanao is the sixth Constituent University of the University of the Philippines System. It is the only Constituent University of UP that was created by law (Republic Act 7889). Its vision is expressed in the word, EXCELLENCE. This is an acronym that stands for, “EXCEL in Leadership, Education, Nationalism, Cultural sensitivity, and Environmental nurturance”.

The mission of UP Mindanao is to lead in providing affordable quality education, scholarly research, and responsive and relevant extension services to diverse, marginalized and deserving sectors in Mindanao and neighboring regions through its programs in the sciences and the arts, inculcating a passion for excellence, creative thinking, and nationalism in the context of cultural diversity in a global community.

UP Mindanao offers a broad choice in degree programs with the best faculty available and a supportive and nurturing environment. UP Mindanao offers all these in order to equip its students with skills to truly make them an offering to the people of Mindanao or wherever their future careers may carry them. The College of Science and Mathematics (CSM) offers BS Biology, BS Food Technology, BS Computer Science, BS Applied Mathematics, and MS Food Science. The College of Humanities and Social Sciences (CHSS) offers BS Anthropology, BS Architecture, BA Communication and Media Art, BA English in Creative Writing, and Diploma in Exercise and Sports Science. The School of Management (SOM) offers BS Agribusiness Economics and the graduate degree programs, Master in Management, Diploma/Master of Arts in Urban and Regional Planning, and PhD by Research.

The UP Mindanao research and development programs are aligned with the directions of the UP System and that of the Philippines’ medium and long-term development plans. These directions are in the areas of science and technology. UP Mindanao is a recipient of various grants for the development of its science and technology capabilities. The most prominent set of grants at present is from the Department of Science and Technology (DOST) and Commission on Higher Education (CHED).

UP Mindanao has state-of-the-art laboratories and equipment which makes learning an exciting and fun-filled experience for students. The laboratory services are intended to also serve other higher educational institutions and constituencies in Mindanao and in the region.

UP Mindanao is located in Barangay Mintal, in a setting that is unique in the booming Davao City. It is surrounded by a landscape that is free of intrusive urban and commercial distractions. It envisions itself to be a “green university town” in the future, one that retains its natural environment and uses “green” technologies.



University of the Philippines Visayas

The University of the Philippines Visayas is the fourth autonomous university of the University of the Philippines System which was established by the Board of Regents on May 31, 1979 to cater to the development of fisheries and marine resources in the country. As an academic institution, UP Visayas aims to “develop excellence and leadership in fisheries and marine education, research and extension in the country, expand opportunities for professional and technical training to the rural areas, contribute to professional and technical training for the rural areas, contribute to rural development through relevant instructional, research and extension programs in management, education, engineering, health and related fields, and preserve and enhance the national and Visayan cultural heritage”.

UP Visayas has three campuses located in Miagao, Iloilo (Main Campus), Iloilo City, and Tacloban City. The UPV - Miagao Campus consists of two colleges and one school: the College of Fisheries and Ocean Sciences (CFOS), the College of Arts and Sciences (CAS), and the School of Technology (SoTech). As of May 2018, the flagship campus of UPV is still considered as a Center of Excellence in Fisheries by the Commission on Higher Education (CHED). Meanwhile, the UPV - Iloilo City Campus is the home of the College of Management which consists of two departments, namely, the Department of Accounting and the Department of Management. The college also offers a Diploma in Urban and Regional Planning in collaboration with the School of Urban and Regional Planning (SURP) at UP Diliman. Lastly, the UPV - Tacloban College is a regional unit of the UP System that was established in the Eastern Visayas Region (Region VIII).

In addition, UP Visayas has 7 research and training centers: the UPV Marine Biological Station in Taklong Island, Guimaras, the Batan Mariculture Center in Batan, Aklan, the Freshwater Mariculture Center and the Regional Research Center in UPV Miagao Campus, the Brackishwater Aquaculture Center in Leganes, Iloilo, the Center for Western Visayan Studies in UPV Iloilo City Campus, and the Leyte-Samar Heritage Center in UPV Tacloban College.
